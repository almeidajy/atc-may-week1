Weekly Report

English At Young Age Program

May 5-9, 2025

Report compiled by: Almeida José

Executive Summary

This report summarizes the activities conducted in the English At Young Age program for the week of May 5-9, 2025. The program is divided into morning and afternoon sessions with different focus areas and student groups.

Morning sessions primarily focused on schedules, time expressions, and daily routines, while afternoon sessions covered the English alphabet and time-related vocabulary. Various teaching methodologies were employed, including informative, illustrative, collaborative, and participatory approaches.

Despite some attendance challenges due to reported climate issues, significant progress was made in both curriculum areas.

Morning Session (8:00 AM)

Teacher: José

Theme: Talking about schedule and Time
Introduction:

Questions to make when asking about time:

  • Q: "What time is it?"
  • A: "It is 2 O'clock"
  • A: "It is half past 3"
  • A: "It's quarter to 5"
Development:

Activity 2: Make your Schedule

  • Create a daily schedule using times and activities
  • Draw a chart on a piece of paper
  • Write the time in the chart
  • Try to add more activities
  • Try to make a chart for a week
Learning Objectives:

Understanding and practice asking and answering questions about times and drawing schedules.

Weekly Insights & Recommendations

Key Observations

  • Morning sessions consistently had lower attendance compared to afternoon sessions.
  • Climate change was cited multiple times as a reason for student absence.
  • Practical and participatory methods engaged students effectively.
  • Progress in alphabet learning and time expressions was consistent throughout the week.
  • Maintenance issue identified with one table in the classroom.

Recommendations

  • Investigate causes of consistent absences, particularly in the morning class.
  • Consider rescheduling morning sessions if climate issues persist.
  • Repair the damaged table reported by Teacher Verónica.
  • Continue with multi-sensory teaching approaches that have proven effective.
  • Consider merging small classes if attendance continues to be low.
  • Follow up with consistently absent students to improve attendance.
